Smoked Salmon Sushi Recipe

Description

Smoked salmon sushi is a fusion of traditional Japanese sushi and a rich, smoky twist. This sushi is easy to prepare and offers a delicate balance of flavors: savory smoked salmon, tangy sushi rice, and fresh vegetables. Perfect for an elegant appetizer or a delightful main dish, it's as beautiful as it is delicious.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of sushi rise
  • 2 Â½ cups water
  • 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 6 sheets of nori (seaweed)
  • 200g (7 oz) smoked salmon
  • 1 cucumber (julienned)
  • 1 avocado
  • Soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi (for serving)

Steps

  1. Prepare the sushi rise
    • Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
    • Combine rice and water in a pot. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer on low heat for 18 minutes. Let it rest for 10 minutes, covered.
    • In a small bowl, mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved. Gently fold this mixture into the cooked rice and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Prepare the sushi rolling station
    • Lay a bamboo sushi mat on a flat surface and cover it with plastic wrap. Place a sheet of nori shiny side down on the mat.
  3. Assemble the sushi rolls
    • Wet your hands to prevent sticking, then spread a thin, even layer of rice over the nori, leaving about 1 inch at the top edge uncovered.
    • Arrange smoked salmon, cucumber, and avocado slices in a line across the center of the rice.
    • Using the bamboo mat, roll the sushi tightly, starting from the bottom edge. Press gently to shape the roll.
  4. Cut the rolls
    • Use a sharp knife dipped in water to cut each roll into 6-8 pieces.
