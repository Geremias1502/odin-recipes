Milanesa Recipe

A plate of Milanesas

Description

Milanesa is a beloved classic of Argentine cuisine, a breaded and fried or baked thin slice of beef. Often served with mashed potatoes, salad, or fries, it's a comfort food that holds a special place in every Argentine home. Perfectly crispy on the outside and tender inside, Milanesa is both simple and delicious.

Ingredients

  • 6 thin slices of beef (preferably from the top round, eye of round, or sirloin)
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 garlic cloves (finely choped)
  • Fresh Parsley (to taste, finely choped)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • Breadcrumbs (as needed)
  • Oil (for frying or drizzling if baking)

Steps

  1. Prepare the meat
    • Clean the slices of beef, trimming excess fat if necessary, and tenderize them slightly with a meat mallet or the flat side of a knife.
  2. Prepare the egg mixture
    • In a bowl, whisk the eggs with the chopped garlic, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  3. Bread the meat
    • Dip each slice of beef into the egg mixture, ensuring it's well-coated.
    • Then coat it with breadcrumbs, pressing firmly to ensure the crumbs stick evenly.
  4. Cooking options
    • Fried
      • Heat enough oil in a frying pan to submerge the slices. Fry each milanesa until golden brown on both sides. Drain on paper towels.
    • Baked
      • Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F). Arrange the breaded beef slices on a lightly oiled baking tray or lined with parchment paper. Drizzle a little oil on top and bake for about 20 minutes, flipping them halfway through.
